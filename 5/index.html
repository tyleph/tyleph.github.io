1.2.3 Denoising Pure Noise

Analysis: When the model attempts to denoise pure noise (which it wasn't trained on), it fails to generate distinct digits. Instead, we can see that the outputs look like blurry, average digits. This happens because the model is trained with an L2 (MSE) loss, so when faced with ambiguous inputs like pure noise, the optimal strategy to minimize error is to predict the "average" of the digits dataset rather than committing to a specific digit.

Sampling without Learning Rate Decay Scheduler

Impact of removing the scheduler: The standard scheduler decays the learning rate to refine the model's weights as it converges. Without it, the model may oscillate or fail to settle into a good minimum. To compensate for this and achieve the results on the left, I lowered the constant learning rate from 1e-2 to 1e-3, which I found kept training stable while finding a good "sweet spot" to produce the same results.
